Design – Making ideas happen

Put on your thinking cap and get ready with some pencils. It’s time to do some sketches.

User Scenarios and Task Flows

Construct user scenarios and storyboarding based on the personas derived to capture the user interactions and task flows on the system. This can provide a valuable insight on task completion and challenges faced by the user which helps in deriving the design solutions. Review and align with the business architecture design to ensure the business objectives can be achieved.

 

Information Architecture and Wireframing

Before developing the structure of your page, the sitemap and information architecture will be created. One of the common methods is through card sorting.

Once we’ve laid the groundwork, it is time to convert all of them into a tangible visual representation of the product. The simplest form of designing a user interface is through wireframing. With wireframes, you may present the user interface design to the stakeholders and development team before converting them into low or high fidelity prototypes.

 

Visual Design

The visual design does not only give a product a character but also an identity. Subtle details like color accuracy and font-sizes have to be accurately captured to ensure consistency and effective visual design. Typically, these are referred to as styleguides. Take note of how colour and contrast plays a part in how the user interface can be presented. More often than not, objects with higher contrast to their surroundings draws the attention of the user much like how red color often stands out from the user’s viewpoint. The visual hierarchy of your design is important to guide the user’s attention as they navigate through the webpage.
