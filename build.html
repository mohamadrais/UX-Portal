Build- Creating Experience that Matters

"Design is not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works." – Steve Jobs.

Let’s build and learn.

System Requirement Statements

Now that the task flows, information architecture and wireframe design is agreed with the stakeholders, capture them into the System Requirements Statement (SRS). The SRS will be a living document which will be updated throughout the project lifecycle.

 

Iteration Planning: Prototyping and User Validation

With the specifications defined, plan the development of low and high fidelity prototype through agile methodology and allow time to constantly engage users for usability test to gather immediate feedback in building the right product that meets user needs.

Prototyping

  1. Low Fidelity Prototype Start drafting your interview questions and estimate the ideal sampling size in conducting the interviews so as to draw an accurate result representing the overall user’s perspective and persona. Keep in mind to ask users what they think and feel, not what should be done. Prototype with limited functionality and constructed to depict page layouts, concepts and visual designs with the aim to gather early user feedback through minimal development effort
  2. High Fidelity Prototype Prototype which is close to a final product with functionality and can be used thoroughly for usability test. Keep in mind to create a responsive prototype.

User Validation

Once you are happy with the prototype developed, it’s time to find out if the users are.

Select the most appropriate usability test methods (A/B testing, Eye Tracking, remote testing) depending on the scope and progress of the prototype. The UX consultant can act as the moderator while the designers and developers can observe the usability test – Plan this well so as not to alert the users that the whole world is observing.

Capture the test results and measure against the benchmarked data on usability datalogger and gradually track for improvements. These data will be analyze and synthesize into tangible recommendations and strategies for next iteration or phase.

 

Final Usability Evaluation

Complete the build of the product based on the SRS along with functionality and integration test with external interfaces. When it is ready to deploy, conduct a final usability evaluation on the product.
